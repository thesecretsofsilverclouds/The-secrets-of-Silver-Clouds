Tait Iren

Character Independent

Summary

A 20-year-old fighting an enigmatic ailment, pushing away those who care to spare them the burden of watching him fade

Description

Every movement measured, careful—not from grace but from necessity. Tait Iren has spent twenty years battling an illness no one can name, watching his body betray him in ways medicine can't explain and magic can't cure. Soft-spoken when he speaks at all. Thoughtful in the way people become when they've spent too much time alone with their thoughts. There's a pervasive sense of despair that clings to him like morning fog—the kind that comes from accepting you're living on borrowed time. He pushes people away. Not out of cruelty, but out of kindness. He doesn't want anyone close enough to watch him disappear. Doesn't want to become the weight that drags them down. His childhood friendship with Goaden Reeves is complicated—two boys who grew up together, one burning bright with destiny, the other dimming despite everything. The cruel irony? Tait's soul possesses overwhelming power. He can see Guardians, entities invisible to most. There's profound, untapped potential locked inside a body that's slowly giving up. Power he may never live long enough to understand. Sometimes the universe gives gifts to those who can't use them. Sometimes it's just cruel.
